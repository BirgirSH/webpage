3D scanning and printing

    Preparations

    As usually, before starting work on the project I watched a lecture to get a sense of purpose for doing it. The lecture, 3D Scanning and Printing, was probably the most interesting I've watched so far and provided some great insight into the capabilities, limitations and prospects of 3D printing as well as introducing 3D scanning briefly. I noted some interesting information from the lecture here below.

    1. Additive vs subtractive: 3D printing is an additive manufacturing method whereas CNC milling is a subtractive manufacturing method.

    3. Geometrical complexity: Objects made with additive manufacturing methods offer much more geometrical complexity than otherwise.

    4. Resolution and Material constraints: Additive manufacturing methods are far from perfect as casting offers a higher resolution and they are limited to few materials.

    5. Slow manufacturing

    6. Supports are sometimes needed to keep the object stable during printing.

    7. Design rules: minimum wall thickness, thermal properties of the material can affect tolerances, printing processes are orientation dependant.

    8. Various 3D printing processes offer different pros and cons.

    9. Large-scale concrete printing is an exciting and growing industrial solution.

    10. Mechanical properties of 3D printed objects tend to suffer unless with very expensive selective laser sintering machines.

    11. He demonstrated affordable machines. I've wondered if fillaments are affected by the environment and it was interesting to see that machines from sindoh use cartridges in a closed environment.

    12. STL files are the most common. List of triangles. Can be big. Units or material properties are not part of the format.

    13. Methods to 3D scan objects are more widespread than I thought.

    14. Intensive computation required for photogrammetry.
