Final project

    Goal

    After extensive brainstorming my group decided that the final project would be to design and create a subwoofer inspired by Bowers and Wilkins' Nautilus design as introduced in project 1. It came as no surprise that many enthusiasts have done comparable projects before, for example Nickolae's 3D printed, upside-down version from which we will be drawing heavy inspiration from.

    Our version, however, will utilize molding and casting techniques to create the spiral shell housing. The bracket to hold the subwoofer will be cut from wood with a CNC machine similarly to Nickolae's project but will incorporate a pressfit design in some manner. Finally, the feet suports will be 3D printed and the whole assembly screwed into place with from-the-shelf screws.

    Concept

    To begin with, I made a couple of simple sketches that, although far from accurate and might not represent the final product, will lay the necessary conceptual groundwork for the CAD design.

    CAD Modelling

    The first part that was modelled was the bracket as it is easier to parameterize in case we would decide to choose another subwoofer.

    Then I started to model the most complex component of this design, the spiral shell. I began with inserting a picture of a nautilus speaker into my workspace.

    Using the picture as a reference, I created a sketch and followed the geometry closely using the spline tool.

    After a short while I had completed the spiral profile.

    As expected, the scale of the sketch was not correct. Using the measure tool I measured the length of the straight line (the diameter around the speaker) and then used the scale feature on the entire sketch so that the straight line would have a length of 127 mm (5 in).

    After fixing the scale I created a new sketch on the same plane as the spiral profile and drew a 2-point circle that would serve as the "beginning" of the spiral. A line was also drawn in the midle of the circle.

    Using the revolve feature on half of the circle area from the previous sketch, I created a new body. Only a 90° angle was chosen as it is easier to see the interior of the spiral shell if it is not closed.

    Similarly, the "end" of the spiral shell was created that would finally be cut.

    Using the loft feature on these two half-circle surfaces and selecting the spiral sketch as the rails unfortunately resulted in an error message that advised to do this in multiple steps. I then proceded to create 20 new planes to draw more circles onto.

    Beginning with drawing one circle (maybe I should have begun with creating one plane also) I again tried the loft feature on these two very close sketches, again selecting the spiral sketch as the rails.

    The result was a partial success. The loft feature was successfully created but it dit not follow the rails correctly.

    After a couple very strenuous hours of trying to make this work and failing, I decided to start a new design. This time I would begin with defining a parameter, DriverDiameter, and setting its inital value as 127mm.
